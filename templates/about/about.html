About Dagkot

Dagkot is a Bisayan term which means to ignite or light up with fire. A popular ritual amongst Filipinos is to go to church, light up one or more candles, then offer a prayer.

In the Visayan region, this ritual is commonly known as "dagkot", which means "to light a candle and offer a prayer." Almost all churches in the Philippines have a "dagkotanan", a place where people light up candles and pray for their intentions.

People usually do the act of dagkot when celebrating birthdays, fiestas, thanks giving, or to commemorate loved ones that passed away. They believe that lighting a candle and offering a prayer would bring good luck and more blessings.

Everyday, lots of people want to go to church to dagkot, but are unable to do so because of their busy schedules or other personal reasons.

This Web application, Virtual Dagkot, allows them to dagkot from the comfort of their home, or even from their offices.
